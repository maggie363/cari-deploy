The Settlement-Netting Model

Zero-Invoicing Friction: Cari collects 1-1.5% service fee from the vendor, tiered by settlement speed (fees based on speed of settlement).

Automated Collection: Fees are deducted directly from the settlement. Vendors never write a check; they simply receive their capital faster, net of Cari's fee and the loyalty rewards.

Strategic DSO Compression

Liquidity Velocity: For a distributor, paying an interchange fee to accelerate payment by 20 days represents significant annualized saving on working capital.

The Outcome: The vendor is more liquid, the restaurant is rewarded, and Cari captures the spread.

Vendor-Funded Loyalty

Redirected Spend: The rewards earned by restaurants for early payments, enabled by Cari, are funded by the vendor, not Cari.

Capital Reallocation: We empower vendors to redirect "leaky" dollars — previously lost to rebates, discounts, and collections — into a high-signal loyalty engine that drives repeat order volume.

Case Study: Tacombi

The Restaurant

The Partner: Tacombi is a premier Mexican taqueria and CPG brand with 20+ current units and a massive 75+ location expansion roadmap.

The Challenge: Managing $25M in annual COGS across Tier-1 vendors (Chefs' Warehouse, Pat LaFrieda) while scaling rapidly requires extreme capital efficiency.

The Cari Impact:
$500,000+ in annual bottom-line savings via 2% rewards. Capital velocity dramatically improved.

The Vendor

The Problem: Traditional 24-day payment terms (average 41 DSO) force vendors to carry $1.75M–$2M in standing receivables for Tacombi alone. They are "unwilling bankers" with trapped liquidity.

The Growth Tax: As Tacombi scales, the vendor's capital strain increases. Under the old model, a customer's success becomes a vendor's liability.

The Cari Intervention: Immediate settlement eliminates DSO. Collections fully automated.
